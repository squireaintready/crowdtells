Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Crowdtells (“we”, “us”) is a free news service that reads prediction markets and the press together. This policy explains what we collect, why, and your choices. We keep data collection to the minimum needed to run the site.

What we collect

  • Account info — if you sign in (Google or email), we store your email and, for Google, your display name and avatar URL, to attribute your comments. We never see your Google password.
  • Your contributions — comments, likes, and claim votes you post, with their timestamps.
  • Saved stories & followed topics — when you are signed in, these sync to your account so they follow you across devices. Signed out, they stay only in your browser.
  • Theme — your light/dark choice is stored only in your browser (localStorage) and never leaves your device.

We do not sell your data, and we do not run advertising trackers.

Analytics & cookies

We measure how the site is used to understand what readers find useful — never to identify you. We honor your browser's Do Not Track signal: with it on, neither Google Analytics nor PostHog loads at all.

  • Cloudflare Web Analytics — privacy-first and cookieless. It counts page views and referrers without cookies, without cross-site tracking, and without ever identifying you.
  • Google Analytics 4 — aggregate usage analytics. It sets first-party cookies (e.g. _ga) to gauge new vs. returning visits; we do not link it to advertising or Google Ads. You can opt out with the Google Analytics opt-out add-on or by blocking cookies. This is the only cookie the site sets — everything else (your theme, signed-out saves) stays in your browser's localStorage.
  • PostHog — product analytics that helps us see how features are used (do readers reach the end of a story, make a Call, join a discussion) so we can improve them. It is cookieless — it keeps an anonymous id in your browser's localStorage, not a cookie — and is hosted in the US. We do not collect what you type (comments, search, and form fields are excluded), and we never sell your data or use it for advertising. If you are signed in, your activity is linked to your account so we can understand the signed-in experience; signed out, it stays anonymous. We may record anonymized, replayed sessions to diagnose usability problems, with all text you enter masked. Do Not Track, or blocking posthog.com, turns it off entirely.

Service providers

We rely on a small set of providers to operate:

  • Supabase — authentication and the database storing accounts and comments.
  • Google — optional sign-in (Google OAuth) and Google Analytics (aggregate usage stats; see Analytics & cookies above).
  • Cloudflare — static site hosting (Cloudflare Pages) and privacy-first, cookieless Web Analytics.
  • Polymarket, Kalshi, Google News, Groq — public market data, news headlines, and AI-generated briefings. These power the content; they do not receive your personal data.

Your choices & rights

  • You can edit or delete any comment you’ve posted.
  • You can export your data or permanently delete your account from the account menu (or by contacting us). Deletion removes your profile, comments, likes, votes, saved stories, and topic interests, and unsubscribes your email — it cannot be undone.
  • You can clear local preferences anytime by clearing your browser storage.
